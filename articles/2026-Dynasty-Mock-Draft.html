2026 Dynasty Mock Draft: Round 1 (Superflex)

A 2026 rookie mock draft for Superflex dynasty leagues, breaking down the top 12 prospects and the fringe Round 1 names who could climb.

Dynasty football managers always love this time of year, getting your hands on young talent with all the potential is what managers dream of. Whether you’re at the beginning of your rebuild, looking to make yourself a contender, or write your ticket back to the crown, what you do in your rookie draft matters.

It can carry you to the top, or sink you to the bottom, the impact felt with the choice you make is felt for years.

With that in mind, we’ve put together a mock draft for the 2026 rookie class, focusing on the top 12 prospects who we believe have the best chance to make an immediate impact in Superflex dynasty leagues. We’ll also highlight some fringe Round 1 names who could climb based on team needs and draft capital.

1. Jeremiyah Love (Notre Dame) – RB

Profile: Explosive feature back with home-run ability

Strengths:

  • Elite acceleration and burst through the hole
  • Excellent vision identifying cutback lanes
  • Natural pass catcher with soft hands and route ability
  • Dangerous in space with long-speed to finish runs
  • Capable of handling full three-down workload

Projection: Top 10 pick

NFL Comp: Jahmyr Gibbs (lite)

2. Carnell Tate (Ohio State) – WR

Profile: Safest WR in the class

Strengths:

  • Elite contested catch ability (~85%+)
  • Advanced route running and leverage understanding
  • Strong hands and reliability in traffic
  • Consistently wins on third downs
  • High football IQ and positional awareness

Projection: Top 15 pick

NFL Comp: Keenan Allen / Chris Godwin

3. Jordyn Tyson (Arizona State) – WR

Profile: High-upside WR1 archetype

Strengths:

  • Explosive vertical threat with big-play ability
  • Strong after-the-catch production
  • Natural ability to track the ball deep
  • Creates chunk plays and scoring opportunities

Concerns:

  • Injury history

Projection: Top 20 pick

NFL Comp: Jerry Jeudy / Stefon Diggs

4. Makai Lemon (USC) – WR

Profile: Technician / separator

Strengths:

  • Elite route runner with sharp breaks and footwork
  • Creates consistent separation at all levels
  • Strong agility and change-of-direction ability
  • Efficient production per target
  • Reliable underneath and intermediate weapon

Projection: Round 1

NFL Comp: Amon-Ra St. Brown (lite)

5. Fernando Mendoza (Indiana) – QB

Profile: High-upside, system-driven QB

Strengths:

  • Accurate in short and intermediate areas
  • Quick decision-maker in structured offenses
  • Efficient at moving the chains
  • Operates timing-based passing concepts well

Concerns:

  • Limited arm strength and deep ball ceiling
  • Production tied heavily to system

Projection: Number 1 Overall Pick

NFL Comp: Jared Goff / Matt Ryan

6. KC Concepcion (Texas A&M) – WR

Profile: Dynamic offensive weapon

Strengths:

  • Elite run-after-catch ability
  • Explosive in open space
  • Versatile usage (slot, backfield, motion)
  • Special teams value as returner
  • Quick acceleration and lateral agility

Concerns:

  • Drops
  • Size limitations

Projection: Early/Mid Round 2

NFL Comp: Zay Flowers

7. Kenyon Sadiq (Oregon) – TE

Profile: Elite athlete / mismatch tight end

Strengths:

  • Rare speed for the position
  • Dynamic after the catch
  • Versatile alignment (slot, inline, H-back)
  • Creates mismatches vs linebackers and safeties
  • Explosive play potential

Concerns:

  • Drops
  • Needs refinement vs physical defenders

Projection: Late Round 1

NFL Comp: Evan Engram / Vernon Davis

8. Omar Cooper Jr. (Indiana) – WR

Profile: Physical, versatile WR

Strengths:

  • Strong hands and physical catch style
  • Excellent run-after-catch ability
  • Wins through contact
  • Versatile alignment across formations
  • Plays with toughness and consistency

Projection: Late Round 1– Early Round 2

NFL Comp: Deebo Samuel (lite)

9. Jadarian Price (Notre Dame) – RB

Profile: Physical runner with burst and contact balance

Strengths:

  • Strong downhill running style
  • Good contact balance through traffic
  • Explosive when hitting open lanes
  • Reliable between-the-tackles presence
  • Shows ability to handle volume carries

Concerns:

  • Limited receiving production
  • Can be inconsistent with vision

Projection: Late Round 1 – Round 2

NFL Comp: Aaron Jones

10. Eli Stowers (Vanderbilt) – TE

Profile: Athletic TE (former QB)

Strengths:

  • High football IQ from QB background
  • Finds space in zone coverage
  • Strong run-after-catch ability
  • Reliable intermediate target
  • Positional versatility

Projection: Round 2-3

NFL Comp: Mark Andrews (lite)

11. Denzel Boston (Washington) – WR

Profile: Big-bodied possession WR

Strengths:

  • Elite size and catch radius
  • Reliable red zone target
  • Strong body control
  • Consistent hands
  • Physical play style

Concerns:

  • Limited top-end speed
  • Struggles creating separation

Projection: Late Round 1

NFL Comp: Mike Evans

12. Ty Simpson (Alabama) – QB

Profile: Efficient distributor

Strengths:

  • High completion percentage and accuracy
  • Protects the football
  • Comfortable operating within structure
  • Good timing and rhythm passer
  • Shows poise under pressure

Concerns:

  • Injury history
  • Limited high-end arm talent

Projection: Day 1 – Day 2 Pick

NFL Comp: Brock Purdy

Fringe Round 1 – Depending on Need

These are the names just outside the top 12 who still have a strong chance to work their way into the back of Round 1 depending on league format, draft capital, and roster construction.

Mike Washington Jr. (Arkansas) – RB

Profile: Explosive, one-cut runner with big-play ability

Strengths:

  • Excellent burst through the line with quick acceleration
  • Strong vision in zone schemes, identifies cutback lanes well
  • Capable of creating explosive plays at the second level
  • Shows natural instincts as a runner with decisive cuts
  • Solid balance allows him to run through arm tackles

Concerns:

  • Limited proven workload as a full feature back
  • Needs development in pass protection
  • Receiving ability still developing

Projection: Day 2–3

NFL Comp: D’Andre Swift

Emmett Johnson (Nebraska) – RB

Profile: Instinctive playmaker

Strengths:

  • Strong vision with ball in hands
  • Natural playmaking instincts
  • Useful in passing game
  • Versatile offensive usage

Concerns:

  • Route running needs development
  • Limited separation ability

Projection: Late Round 3-Early Round 5 Pick

NFL Comp: Antonio Gibson

Chris Bell (Louisville) – WR

Profile: Big-bodied possession receiver

Strengths:

  • Strong hands and ability to win contested catches
  • Physical frame allows him to box out defenders
  • Reliable target in short and intermediate areas
  • Effective red zone weapon
  • Shows toughness over the middle

Concerns:

  • Lacks elite separation ability
  • Limited top-end speed

Projection: Day 2–3

NFL Comp: A.J. Brown / Deebo Samuel

Jonah Coleman (Washington) – RB

Profile: Compact, physical runner with versatility

Strengths:

  • Excellent contact balance and low center of gravity
  • Strong vision and patience behind blocks
  • Effective in short-yardage situations
  • Capable pass catcher out of the backfield
  • Consistent chain mover

Concerns:

  • Lacks elite breakaway speed
  • Limited big-play ability

Projection: Day 3

NFL Comp: David Montgomery

Zachariah Branch (USC) – WR

Profile: Dynamic playmaker and elite athlete

Strengths:

  • Elite speed and acceleration
  • Dangerous in space with elite YAC ability
  • Immediate impact as a return specialist
  • Versatile usage (screens, motion, deep shots)
  • Can change the game with one touch

Concerns:

  • Size limitations
  • Needs refinement as a route runner

Projection: 2nd Round Pick

NFL Comp: Tank Dell
